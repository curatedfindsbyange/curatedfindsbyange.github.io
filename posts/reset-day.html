A Perfect Reset Day

January 16, 2026

Like everyone else, life gets loud.

Bills to pay, a house that never quite stays clean, family, responsibilities — and lately, a brand new world I’m learning how to navigate: Pinterest.

I’ve been learning something new every single day. A lot, actually. And yesterday, I realized I needed to pause.

To reset.

A reset looks different for everyone. For me, it’s not productivity or routines or perfectly planned mornings.

Honestly? It’s staying in bed, watching TV, and letting my brain go quiet for a while.

I’m not proud of this, but I love Hallmark movies. I don’t know why. I’m usually not into poorly acted, overly predictable films — but something about them feels safe. Familiar. Soft. Maybe it’s the most basic part of me. I’ve accepted it.

Reset also looks like taking care of my plants.

This is a new thing for me. I used to kill plants. Constantly. Now I kill fewer of them — which feels like progress.

I spend time pruning, watering, learning how to propagate. I know I don’t need to check the roots five times a day while they sit in a jar of water… but I still do. Every time I walk into the kitchen, I look. Waiting for that one strand to grow.

It makes me happy.

I’m not a journaling person. I’ve tried. Even my therapist suggested it. It never really stuck.

But maybe this is my version of it.

A place to slow down. To notice how space, light, texture, and quiet actually affect how I feel.

This is what I want The Neutral Edit to be about. Not perfect homes — but spaces that support real life.

This is my first post. And if you’re here, welcome.

— Ang
